ABOUT RIGHTS NIGHTS

Rights Nights Mission Statement

Rights Nights is a 5 night Human Rights speaker series leading up to Human Rights Day starting on Monday 5 December through to Friday 9 December. Each night focuses on a different facet of human rights in Australia, in 2016 these are:

  1. the rights of Indigenous Australians;
  2. Privacy rights;
  3. Women's health rights;
  4. Disability rights; and
  5. Refugee rights

These nightly events will bring together speakers from not-for-profit organisations, academia, advocacy groups, corporates and the public sector to pursue three key objectives:

  1. Providing attendees with information about the status of each of these rights in Australia
  2. Empowering attendees to advocate for each right, by providing them with ways to promote change
  3. Equipping attendees with tangible opportunities to put this knowledge into action by linking them with organisations advocating for change in the area.

Our Team

Suzie Wnukowska-Mtonga

Suzie Wnukowska-Mtonga is Founder and Managing Director of Rights Nights, and Legal Counsel at Hewlett Packard Enterprise. Suzie has always been passionate about Human Rights. She is a volunteer with the Red Cross, having helped set up programs in both the ACT and in NSW, and also regularly volunteers for the Refugee Advice and Casework Service (RACS). Suzie has been recognised for her contribution to pro bono and social corporate responsibility by being announced as a finalist for the 2016 ACC Australian In-house Lawyer Awards for Excellence in Corporate Social Responsibility. 

Kedar Vishwanathan

Kedar Vishwanathan is a Director at Rights Nights and is a Juris Doctor candidate at University of New South Wales, as well as a paralegal at Colins Biggers and Paisley. Prior to studying law, Kedar worked as a strategy consultant in Rio Tinto's Economics and Markets team. Kedar has a keen interest in privacy rights and corporate social responsibility, having volunteered for a number of NGOs in India. Kedar also holds a PhD from the University of Sydney in Modern Indian Art and Cultural History.

Teela Reid

Teela Reid is a Director of Rights Nights and the current Tipstaff to the Honourable Justice Lucy McCallum of the New South Wales Supreme Court.
